Techsmiths' Skillsets

Versatility

Most businesses require staff that can understand the full lifecycle, and transition between different roles as required. Managing a small project, setting up a CI pipeline, designing a new page, writing high quality code, system testing a product – we’d expect our technical staff to be able to do any of those, and it makes resourcing far more difficult if all of your staff are one-dimensional. We believe in truly cross-functional teams, and that comes from a diverse skill set. All that being said, the core competency/role is still Software Engineer!

Soft Skills

Seems small, but makes a huge difference to how successful delivery team members are. We don't just select candidates based on their potential as a developer but what other expertise they can bring to the table. That being said, we consider it an essential part of training to bring out the best in people and install some great habits. This is all achieved by better diversity of recruitment – something we take very seriously, and have strong supporting networks to help with.

Genuine Understanding

The understanding is far more important than the ability to recall exactly how to do X or Y from the top of your head. Everyone has access to Stack Overflow, Google, etc. whilst they work, but those tools aren’t good at explaining the big picture, or why particular approaches are better than others. The most valuable employees are the ones who understand the objectives, understand requirements that might not be explicitly listed, and can use their own judgement and experience to research the best way of implementing the detail.
